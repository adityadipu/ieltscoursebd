The 11 Golden Tips

Tip 1: Context

Map questions usually appear in Section 2. It's often one speaker talking about a general interest topic (farm, museum, park).

Tip 2: Visual Check (20s)

Use the 20 seconds before audio to check: How many buildings? What are the names? Where are the entrances and roads?

Tip 3: Read Title

The title predicts the content. "City Museum" means you'll hear about rooms and exhibitions. "Park" means paths and trees.

Tip 4: Labels

Use existing labels as anchors. If you see "Library", listen for "Next to the library...".

Tip 5: Position Words

Key vocabulary: Opposite, Beyond, Beside, Facing. These are your primary clues.

Tip 6: Question Order

Questions (11, 12, 13) match the audio order. Locate them on the map before listening so you don't get lost.

Tip 7: Find North

Look for the 'N' arrow. If Up is North, then Right is East. This prevents confusion with left/right.

Tip 8: Follow the Tour

Trace the path with your finger/eyes. "We enter the garden, then walk to the lake..." - move your eyes accordingly.

Tip 9: Signposting

Listen for: "First...", "Next...", "Then...", "Finally...". Don't jump around randomly.

Tip 10: Tour Guide Persona

The speaker acts like a guide. Follow their mental journey step-by-step.

Tip 11: Ignore Distractors

Ignore adjectives like "renovated" or "popular". Focus purely on location data.

Strategy

Before listening: Read the titles, look at the buildings, and find the question number.
