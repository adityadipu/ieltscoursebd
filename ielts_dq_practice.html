10 Essential Strategies

01 Analyze the Title

The title predicts the content. "Library Layout" = rooms. "A Machine" = parts. "A Process" = steps.

02 Focus on Gaps

Don't try to understand the whole picture immediately. Find keywords and focus specifically on the parts with gaps.

03 Predict Question Type

Note Completion: Look for grammatical clues (noun, number).
Empty Labels: Usually a name of a component.

04 Follow Audio Order

Answers follow the audio sequence. Do not let your eyes jump around; let the audio guide you.

05 Check Word Limits

Strictly follow instructions (e.g., "No more than two words"). Extra words make the answer wrong.

06 Use Existing Labels

Use words already written in the diagram as "signposts". They help you stay oriented while listening.

07 Keep Moving

If you miss an answer, do not panic. Move immediately to the next question to avoid a chain reaction.

08 Guess if Needed

Never leave a blank. If you missed it, make a logical guess based on the diagram's context.

09 Diagram Language

Listen for location words: At the top, Below, Adjacent to, In the center, On the periphery.

10 Audio Signals

Phrases like "As you can see in the illustration..." are signals to focus hard immediately.
