Pronouns (11 Types)

1. Personal
I, We, You, He, She, It
"He is a good boy."
2. Possessive
Mine, Hers, His, Yours
"This pen is mine."
3. Emphatic
Myself, Himself, Yourself, Themself
"I did this work myself."
4. Reflexive
Myself, Himself, Yourself, Themself
"She herself manages all this."
5. Intensive
Myself, Himself, Yourself, Themself
"I myself done this work."
6. Interrogative
Who? What? Which?
"Who are you?"
7. Relative
Who, What, Which
"The boy who won the match."
8. Demonstrative
This, That, These
"These are rotten mangoes."
9. Indefinite
Some, Any, Many
"Give me something to eat."
10. Distributive
Each, Either, Neither
"Each one of you learn."
11. Reciprocal
Each other, One another
"They help one another."

Primary Auxiliary Verbs

1. Do Verb (Do, Did, Does)

Present (Do/Does)
  • "Do you eat rice?" - "No, I don't."
  • "Do you go to university?" - "Yes, I go."
  • "Does she play well?" - "She plays well."
  • "Does it eat fish?" - "Yes, it eats fish."
  • "Does the Sun shine?" - "Yes, it shines."
Past (Did)
  • "Did you know him?" - "No, I didn't."
  • "Did you eat beef?" - "No, I didn't."
  • "Did they visit there?" - "No, they didn't."
  • "Did Sumon do research?" - "Yes, he did."
Doing (Continuous)
"I am doing my job perfectly." / "She is not doing her job." / "We are doing the right things."

2. Have Verb (Perfect Tense)

TenseExamples
Present"I have eaten rice." / "He has completed his task." / "They haven't finished."
Past"I had eaten rice." / "You had played football."
Future"He will have completed his task." / "We will have dancing on the floor."

Modals & Advanced Usage

1. Can & Could

Can (Ability/Permission)
  • "I can beat you."
  • "Can you close the door?"
  • "It can rain today." (Possibility)
Could (Past/Polite)
  • "Could you close the window?" (Polite)
  • "When I was young, I could run." (Past)
  • "If I were a bird, I could fly." (Imaginary)
